Introduction: Turmoil[]

Turmoil is a Low Class 3 difficulty with a rating of 10.5 placing it in-between Extreme and Terrifying. This difficulty has a Metris rating of |157|, meaning that it is near the end of the High Insane through Bottom-Low Terrifying range. The tier rating for this difficulty is Tier 13.

Turmoil is Humanly Possible, meaning that it can be beaten by anybody that has enough skill to beat it. However, since this difficulty is on the higher end of the spectrum, a lot of jumps have to be precisely done, as well as some very complex mechanisms that have to be figured out and done very quickly. The punishment level is also really high, with most fails sending someone playing a tower of this difficulty down to floor 1.

A victor of a Turmoil difficulty tower would be considered legitimate, unless hacks were seen being used in a recording of someone completing a tower of this difficulty. This is because although this difficulty is Humanly Possible, this does not mean that it is easy. This means that it is prone to hackers/external program users trying to complete a tower of its difficulty.

Completing a Turmoil difficulty tower is an amazing feat in itself, as over 90% of roblox players can’t even pass the first floor of most towers in this difficulty, let alone an entire ten floors. Beating a tower of this difficulty should be considered an achievement by all players, since most tower in this difficulty usually only have a victor amount in the triple digits. To put this into comparison, over 4 billion accounts have been created. Out of those 4 billion accounts, only a few hundred, possibly a few thousand, have completed any tower of this difficulty.

If one wishes to beat a tower of Turmoil difficulty, they’d need to have a lot of skill. This is because there are some long jumps that are in the low double digits. These jumps include 12.55 stud jumps, 10.45 stud wraps, and much more not listed here. All of these jumps will have to be done precisely.

There are some certain techniques that one has to perform in a Turmoil difficulty tower. These tactics include glitch wrapping, corner flip jumps, dance clipping jumps, etcetera. Without the ability to do these techniques, it is impossible to beat a tower of this difficulty.

    After one completes a tower of Turmoil difficulty, they are recommended to keep beating towers of its difficulty. However, they can immediately go on to try and complete a tower of Terrifying difficulty. This, however, is a bad decision, as towers in that range of difficulty require even more precise jumps, timings, etcetera.

    Explanation to Navbox / Notes▼✕
    Main Pages Difficulty Chart, Classes (Category)
    Notes

    The difficulty tabs always open downwards.

    Keep in mind that this navbox was not designed for light mode.

    Note that some of the links are broken or redirects of another page.

    Explanation

    All difficulties are organized by class and spectrum including sub difficulties. Unclassified difficulties are excluded from this rule.

    The Main Difficulty Chart is spilt into two parts: Main Chart and Extended Chart.

    Difficulties with long names will get abbreviated. For example, Ifinite-Ifinite-Easy-Ifinite-Easy-Alpha-Easy-Null-Ifinite: Revamp.

    Some of the chains are incorporated into the main chart. For example, the Aleph Sequence was integrated into the whole Class 11's chart in attempt to improve connectivity.

    Chains with one page are not added to the navbox. The Cat Chain is an example of incorporating all difficulties to form a single page.

    Unions will become regular difficulties and placed on their respective classes.

    Primordial means difficulties that break up the whole spectrum of the Main Difficulty Chart. This consists five difficulties placed at their respective positions.

    Legendary means difficulties earns high respect by the community are ranked as legendary difficulties as they are designed well. These difficulties must be accepted by Nugly in order to become legendary.

    Fabled mean that difficulties define new importance and uniqueness to the Main Difficulty Chart.

    Ethereal is reserved only for Babass dficuklty, and means that the difficulty is more omnipotent than any other difficulty on the Difficulty Chart.

    Former Legendary mean that difficulties used to be legendary are demoted to low position due to quality issues and arguments.

    Unclassified means that the difficulty is not placed any class on the Difficulty Chart. It can range from a few to many classes.

    Unknown means the difficulty has an unknown placement on the difficulty spectrum and does not appear on the Difficulty Chart.
